Nowhere Near

  • My mother always said to me,
  • "Always dare to dream, be free"
  • But that was only after she reminded me,
  • “Watch what you wear, don’t expose anything above your knee.”
  • I was still a baby.
  • As a young girl, I was told,
  • "Be who you want, be bold"
  • But one condition would always hold,
  • “Don't stay out after the sun is gone cold.”
  • I was just eight years old.
  • The alerts, the warnings, the never-ending caution,
  • The rules, the deadlines, all that prohibition,
  • Were always part of the discussion.
  • And I know I am not an exception.
  • The question is not if, but when we will all be in that situation?
  • The numbers are clear:
  • To be a woman is to live in fear.
  • When will things change; when will all this disappear?
  • We wait for it to get better, year after year.
  • But progress seems nowhere near.
